## Product Overview
**One-liner:** Oregon Tour de Outback — a gravel & road cycling event through Oregon's high-desert outback, benefiting Lake County Search and Rescue.
**What it does:** A one-day (weekend-experience) charity cycling event in Lakeview, OR on **June 26, 2027**, with five routes across gravel and road. Riders come for the scenery and challenge; 100% of proceeds fund Lake County Search and Rescue. On-site camping makes it a destination weekend.
**Product category:** Destination gravel/road cycling event — a charity "ride" / gran fondo–style event (how riders search: "Oregon gravel ride," "gravel event," "century ride Oregon").
**Product type:** Ticketed event (registration), non-profit-benefit. Presented by the Lake County Chamber of Commerce.
**Business model:** Registration fees + camping add-on + sponsorships + donations. All proceeds → Lake County Search and Rescue. Registration handled via **BikeReg**. *(This is a gift project — production maximized, but not counted in Dave's earnings. See vault [[Claude Code Skills]].)*

## Target Audience
**Target riders:** Gravel and road cyclists across the Pacific Northwest and beyond (confirm geographic reach?) — from **first-time gravel riders to seasoned century riders**. Draws destination-event travelers willing to make a weekend of it.
**Decision-makers:** The rider themselves (B2C); often decided with a riding buddy, club, or family. Camping appeals to couples/groups making it a trip.
**Primary use case:** Find a memorable, well-organized, scenic ride to train for and travel to — with a feel-good cause attached.
**Jobs to be done:**
- Give me a bucket-list-worthy ride with real scenery and a challenge matched to my level.
- Let me do good (support SAR) by doing something I already love.
- Give me a welcoming, low-intimidation entry into gravel (for newer riders).
**Use cases:**
- Seasoned rider chasing the 105-mile Century or challenging 48-mile gravel.
- First-timer picking the easy 40-mile road or moderate 36-mile gravel.
- Group/club trip: ride + camp on-site + small-town weekend.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Riders want a scenic, well-run destination event that fits their level and means something — not just another training loop from home.
**Why alternatives fall short (confirm?):**
- Big-city gran fondos lack the open-landscape scenery and small-town warmth.
- Free local rides have no event energy, support, or cause.
- Some gravel events feel intimidating or exclusive to newcomers.
**What it costs them:** A weekend and travel — so the ride has to be worth it.
**Emotional tension:** "Will this be worth the trip? Will I fit in / can I handle it?" — answered by route variety + welcome + cause.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Other Oregon / PNW gravel & road events and gran fondos (confirm specific names?) — fall short on the unique high-desert outback scenery + small-town Lakeview hospitality + 100%-to-SAR story.
**Secondary:** Ride locally / self-organized routes — free, but no support, no event experience, no cause.
**Indirect:** Spend the weekend some other way (other races, travel, non-cycling) — TdO competes for discretionary weekend + travel budget.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Genuinely different terrain — Oregon's **rugged high-desert outback**: gravel through sagebrush valleys + wide-open highways with mountain views.
- **Five routes** (Road 40/53/105 mi; Gravel 36/48 mi) — real range for every level, one registration covers any route.
- **100% of proceeds** to Lake County Search and Rescue — the team that protects the very landscapes you ride.
- **On-site camping** (RV + tent) at the Lake County Fairgrounds → a full weekend basecamp.
- **Small-town welcome** — the thing riders love most, kept through a venue/route refresh.
**How we do it differently:** Community-run (Chamber-presented), cause-first, in a place most riders have never ridden.
**Why that's better:** Scenery + challenge + meaning + hospitality in one weekend.
**Why riders choose us:** "Great rides, great fun, for a great cause."
