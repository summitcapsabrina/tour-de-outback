2. Assumption of Risk

I understand that volunteering at the Oregon Tour de Outback 2026 (the “Event”) carries inherent risks of injury, illness, property damage, or death, which I voluntarily accept. These risks include, without limitation:

  • Working near cyclists riding at speed and near moving vehicles (SAG, support, and personal vehicles);
  • Lifting, operating, or being struck by event equipment (tents, signage, tools, coolers, radios, etc.); slip, trip, and fall hazards on uneven or dusty terrain;
  • Exposure to weather (heat, sun, wind, dust, cold, rain) and altitude effects at approximately 4,800 ft;
  • Working in remote, rural locations with limited cell service and extended emergency response times; driving on remote or unpaved roads;
  • Encounters with wildlife, livestock, insects, and snakes;
  • Food and beverage handling and use of stoves, propane, generators, and fuel sources, including allergic reactions and foodborne illness;
  • Communicable illness and other risks inherent to outdoor, multi-day public events in a high-desert environment.

3. Release, Indemnification, Medical Authorization, and Media Release

In consideration of being permitted to volunteer at the Event, I, on behalf of myself and my heirs, executors, administrators, personal representatives, and assigns, release, waive, discharge, and covenant not to sue the Lake County Chamber of Commerce and its officers, directors, employees, volunteers, agents, contractors, sponsors, and Lake County, Oregon (the “Released Parties”) from any claim, demand, action, liability, damage, loss, cost, or expense (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from or related to my participation as a volunteer between June 24 and June 28, 2026, including travel, setup, breakdown, and Event activities, and any resulting injury, illness, death, or property damage, whether caused by the ordinary negligence of any Released Party or otherwise. This release does not apply to claims arising from gross negligence, recklessness, or intentional misconduct to the extent such claims may not be released under Oregon law.

I agree to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Released Parties from any third-party claim arising from my conduct as a volunteer. In the event of injury or illness, I authorize the Released Parties and any first-responder or medical personnel to administer or arrange necessary first aid, treatment, transportation, or hospitalization, and I accept financial responsibility for such treatment.

I grant the Lake County Chamber of Commerce permission to use my name, image, voice, and likeness in photographs, video, audio, livestreams, social media, and other media captured at the Event, for any lawful promotional, marketing, fundraising, journalistic, or sponsor purpose, without further compensation, notice, or approval.

This Waiver and Release is governed by Oregon law; venue lies in Lake County, Oregon. If any provision is unenforceable, the remainder stays in effect.
