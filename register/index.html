What's Included

Every registration includes fully supported aid stations along your route, a post-ride festival with food, drinks, and live music, event swag, and a finisher reward.

You'll also get access to Camp Tour de Outback at the Lake County Fairgrounds, the pre-ride social on Friday evening, and the awards ceremony on Saturday afternoon.

All proceeds benefit Lake County Search and Rescue.

Frequently Asked Questions

What routes are available? +

We offer five route options: two gravel rides (36-mile and 52-mile) and three road rides (40-mile, 53-mile, and 105-mile century). All routes start and finish at the Lake County Fairgrounds in Lakeview, Oregon.

Is there a refund policy? +

Refund policies are managed through BikeReg. Please review the refund terms on the BikeReg registration page before completing your registration. Transfers to other riders may be available — contact us for details.

What should I bring? +

Bring your bike (road or gravel depending on your route), helmet (required), water bottles, cycling kit, sunscreen, and any nutrition you prefer. Aid stations will be stocked with water, electrolytes, and snacks. For camping, bring your own tent/RV, sleeping gear, and supplies.

Are e-bikes allowed? +

Please check the event rules on the BikeReg registration page or contact us directly for the latest e-bike policy.

Is there support on the course? +

Yes! All routes are fully supported with aid stations stocked with water, electrolytes, snacks, and basic mechanical supplies. SAG vehicles will patrol the course and medical support will be available.

Can I change my route after registering? +

Route changes may be available up until event day. Contact us or check BikeReg for options on transferring between routes.
